# Ballzatram Monetization Readiness

## What Ballzatram is

Ballzatram is a living workshop of playable tools, games, prototypes, simulations, oddities, AI toys, and useful little machines. It is not a finished enterprise SaaS suite. The honest promise is access to the lab as it evolves.

Some things are polished. Some are prototypes. Some may break. Everything should be labeled honestly.

## What Lab Pass sells

Lab Pass can safely sell membership and participation:

- Early access to new tools, games, and experiments.
- Full experimental archive.
- Member-only prototypes.
- Monthly medley or drop.
- Saved favorites where supported.
- Suggestions and upvotes for future ideas.
- Behind-the-scenes dev notes.
- Influence over what gets polished next.
- Support for the lab.

The founding target is $10/month.

## What Lab Pass does not sell

Lab Pass must not sell:

- Live trading.
- Professional investment recommendations.
- Verified land acquisition decisions.
- Guaranteed AI output quality.
- Guaranteed AI Edit uptime.
- Unlimited rendering.
- Durable professional workspaces without user-scoped database storage.
- Legal, financial, real estate, or trading advice.

## What can be monetized now

Safe early monetization:

- Membership/support.
- Early access.
- Archive access.
- Votes and suggestions.
- Favorites.
- Monthly drops.
- Member-only prototypes.
- Behind-the-scenes notes.

These are acceptable because the value is participation in the evolving workshop, not a claim that every artifact is production-grade.

## What must wait

Not safe to sell yet:

- Live trading.
- Investment recommendations.
- Verified land acquisition decisions.
- Guaranteed AI Edit uptime or unlimited rendering.
- Durable professional workspaces without user-scoped database storage.
- Legal, financial, real estate, or trading advice.
- Heavy creator workflows without quotas, queues, storage policy, and abuse controls.

Checkout should wait until the membership promise, cancellation terms, privacy posture, and operational boundaries are ready.

## Why unfinished is acceptable if labeled honestly

Unfinished work can be sold as early access when expectations are explicit. A prototype can be valuable as a strange tool, a learning lab, or a voteable direction. It becomes unsafe when the site pretends the prototype is a professional replacement for expert judgment, durable records, or production infrastructure.

Status labels should tell users what to expect before they click.

## Safety boundaries

- Weather Desk remains research and paper mode only.
- Parcel must keep source, truth, verification, and diligence language visible.
- AI Edit must require rights-approved or permissioned media and backend rendering for real exports.
- Quant Library must avoid personalized recommendation language.
- Provider keys, payment secrets, auth secrets, and entitlement logic stay server-side only.
- Static public pages must not imply checkout, membership activation, or paid entitlements exist today.

## Pre-checkout checklist

- Publish final paid terms and cancellation policy.
- Add real contact and support process.
- Add auth and user records.
- Add subscription records and webhook handling.
- Add entitlement checks server-side.
- Add favorites, votes, suggestions, drops, and progress to Postgres.
- Add quotas and rate limits for heavy tools.
- Add object storage and retention policy for uploads/exports.
- Add observability and admin moderation.
- Run legal/privacy review for uploaded media, payments, analytics, and user data.

## Post-checkout checklist

- Monitor paid signup, cancellation, refund, and support flows.
- Watch heavy-job costs daily.
- Track render queue latency and failure rate.
- Audit entitlement bypass attempts.
- Publish monthly drops and dev notes consistently.
- Review suggestions/upvotes for abuse or spam.
- Keep status labels updated when tools mature or regress.
- Add classroom/team/creator tiers only after individual membership is stable.
